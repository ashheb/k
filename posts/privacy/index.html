Privacy From Facebook

  • Sep 24, 2020
  • 5 minutes read

Facebook is one of the most widely used social media networks out there. Almost everyone who has ever browsed the internet has an account on Facebook. People love sharing their pictures with friends and family on Facebook. People share their opinions and discuss a wide range of topics on Facebook Groups, find roommates, sell stuff etc., on Facebook.

Few know Facebook is not just a social media network. A major portion of their earnings is from their Ad network. Facebook is likely on par with Google in terms of tracking users across the web. Their tracking is not limited to just facebook.com or Instagram. Their sphere of influence extends far and wide beyond that.

Disabling or deleting facebook or instagram profile/account is not the end of story sadly. A robust way to stop these trackers is stopping your computer from trying to connect with the facebook servers. And no, it’s not just facebook.com unfortunately.

A group of privacy minded people maintain a list of domains that can be safely added to a blocklist to prevent tracking from facebook. What this does is the following:

  1. Add a domain name to the hosts file (details explained below)
  2. Tell your computer to connect to nowhere instead of those specific domains.

As an example, should you want to block example.com, then in your hosts file, add this entry:

0.0.0.0 example.com

Here, 0.0.0.0 is the IP address to be connected to example.com is the site

So essentially, what this does is, it tells the computer to route requests for example.com to 0.0.0.0 (nowhere :D)

On the bright side, this should not take more than 10 mins the first time. On the flip side, this is a cat and mouse game - the list gets updated, albeit infrequenty, so you have to update too. But the biggest drawback is that these block lists work at the device level - if you have a windows laptop, a mac and a gaming desktop, then you have to do this on all devices.
