Post Two: Running Notes for Mac

  • Sep 19, 2020
  • One minute read

My second post here (well, first, ignoring the Hello World.

This is more of a collection of notes that I’m trying to maintain of some of the commands that I use(d) on my MacBook Pro.

  • Keep an eye on the Charge levels over the course of the past few days (weeks?)
$ pmset -g log | grep -w Charge
  • Get a count of number of battery cylces.
$ system_profiler SPPowerDataType | grep "Cycle Count" | awk '{print $3}'

Output will be an integer. Example: 432

  • Get the status of battery.
$ system_profiler SPPowerDataType | grep "Condition" | awk '{print $2}'

Output will be a text indicating the status of battery. Example: Normal

  • Get more details about battery:
$ system_profiler SPPowerDataType | grep -A3 -B7 "Condition"

Output will be similar to:

    Charge Information:
      Charge Remaining (mAh): 2405
      Fully Charged: No
      Charging: No
      Full Charge Capacity (mAh): 5321
    Health Information:
      Cycle Count: 123
      Condition: Normal
    Battery Installed: Yes
    Amperage (mA): -24
    Voltage (mV): 11852
